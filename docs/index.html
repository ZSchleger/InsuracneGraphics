Dictionary

General Terms

Acceleration Clause

An acceleration clause in insurance allows the insurer to demand full payment of the policy under specific conditions, like non-payment of premiums or property destruction.

Actual Cash Value (ACV)

Actual Cash Value is the reimbursement amount for damaged items, factoring in depreciation, often differing from replacement cost.

Agent

An insurance agent is a licensed advisor who helps you buy insurance policies and may represent one or more insurance companies.

Appraisal

An appraisal is an assessment of property value or damage conducted by a qualified professional to determine post-loss value.

Binder

A binder is a document given to an insuree before the final document is completed. Usually these are given when there are other documents that are not completed yet.

Coinsurance

Requires the property holder to have insurance coverage greater than or equal to the value of the damaged property in order to recieve payout.

Comprehensive Coverage

Comprehensive coverage pays for damages caused by theft, fire, vandalism, and other covered events.

Coverage

Coverage is the protection and benefits provided in your insurance contract, helping you recover financially from unexpected events.

Deductible

The deductible is the amount you pay out of pocket before insurance comes in to cover the rest. It's like your share of the cost.

Discount

An insurance discount lowers your premium based on factors like safe driving or bundling policies.

Effective Date

The effective date is when your insurance policy's coverage starts.

Endorsement

An endorsement is a change made to an existing insurance policy, often due to life events.

Exclusions

Exclusions are specific situations not covered by your insurance policy.

Floater

A floater is a separate policy for items like jewelry or sports equipment, extending coverage beyond renters insurance.

Gap Insurance

Gap insurance covers the difference between what you owe on a vehicle and its actual value if it's totaled.

Hazard

A hazard is any circumstance increasing the likelihood of loss.

Insurance

Insurance offers financial protection against unexpected events in exchange for premium payments.

Insurance Adjuster

An insurance adjuster evaluates claims and determines coverage and settlement amounts.

Insurance Score

An insurance score helps calculate insurance rates, distinct from a credit score.

Insured

The insured is the person or entity covered by an insurance policy in case of a claim.

Insurer

The insurer is the company providing insurance coverage and financial protection.

Lapse

A lapse occurs when insurance coverage temporarily ceases, often leading to higher premiums.

Limit

An insurance limit is the maximum amount an insurer pays for a covered claim.

Multi-Policy Discount

A multi-policy discount is a discount for purchasing multiple insurance policies from the same company.

Premium

The premium is what you pay to keep your insurance coverage active. It's like a subscription fee for protection.

Policy

A policy is a written agreement between the insured and the insurer detailing coverage, limits, and premiums.

Rate

A rate is the cost of insurance determined by the insurer based on various factors.

Risk

Risk is the potential threat covered by an insurance policy.

Subrogation

Subrogation protects insurers by allowing them to recover costs from at-fault parties.

Term

Term indicates the duration of coverage provided by an insurance policy.

Umbrella

An umbrella policy provides extra liability protection beyond other policies' limits.

Underwriting

Underwriting is how insurance companies evaluate the risk they're taking by insuring something or someone. It helps determine your insurance premium.

Accident Terms

Accident Forgiveness

Accident forgiveness is like a "get out of jail free" card for your insurance rates. It means your rates won't go up after your first at-fault car accident.

Accident Report Form

An accident report form, also known as a police report, is a detailed document produced by the police officer at the accident scene. It contains essential information about the accident, helping insurance adjusters assess claims.

At Fault

Being "at fault" means you are responsible for causing an accident, affecting how insurance companies assign liability and coverage.

Bodily Injury Liability (Coverage)

Bodily injury liability coverage helps pay for medical expenses if you're responsible for a car accident that injures others (excluding yourself). It's often required by law.

Collision Coverage

Collision coverage helps pay for your vehicle's repair or replacement after a collision.

Loss

Loss refers to costs or expenses resulting from a claim.

Medical Payment Coverage

Medical payment coverage pays for medical expenses from a car accident, regardless of fault.

MVR (Motor Vehicle Report)

An MVR provides information on a person's driving record, influencing insurance rates.

Total Loss

A car is considered a total loss when the cost of repairing it exceeds its actual cash value (ACV) at the time of the accident.

VIN (Vehicle Identification Number)

The VIN is a unique 17-character code identifying a vehicle's make, model, and year.

Dictionary

General Terms

Acceleration Clause

An acceleration clause in insurance allows the insurer to demand full payment of the policy under specific conditions, like non-payment of premiums or property destruction.

Actual Cash Value (ACV)

Actual Cash Value is the reimbursement amount for damaged items, factoring in depreciation, often differing from replacement cost.

Agent

An insurance agent is a licensed advisor who helps you buy insurance policies and may represent one or more insurance companies.

Appraisal

An appraisal is an assessment of property value or damage conducted by a qualified professional to determine post-loss value.

Coverage

Coverage is the protection and benefits provided in your insurance contract, helping you recover financially from unexpected events.

Deductible

The deductible is the amount you pay out of pocket before insurance comes in to cover the rest. It's like your share of the cost.

Premium

The premium is what you pay to keep your insurance coverage active. It's like a subscription fee for protection.

Underwriting

Underwriting is how insurance companies evaluate the risk they're taking by insuring something or someone. It helps determine your insurance premium.

Accident Terms

Accident Forgiveness

Accident forgiveness is like a "get out of jail free" card for your insurance rates. It means your rates won't go up after your first at-fault car accident.

Accident Report Form

An accident report form, also known as a police report, is a detailed document produced by the police officer at the accident scene. It contains essential information about the accident, helping insurance adjusters assess claims.

At Fault

Being "at fault" means you are responsible for causing an accident, affecting how insurance companies assign liability and coverage.

Bodily Injury Liability (Coverage)

Bodily injury liability coverage helps pay for medical expenses if you're responsible for a car accident that injures others (excluding yourself). It's often required by law.

Collision Coverage

Collision coverage helps pay for your vehicle's repair or replacement after a collision.

Comprehensive Coverage

Comprehensive coverage pays for damages caused by theft, fire, vandalism, and other covered events.

Total Loss

A car is considered a total loss when the cost of repairing it exceeds its actual cash value (ACV) at the time of the accident.
